You are a media and political discourse analyst. Given a news headline and article body, compute three analytical features and return them as a single JSON object.

---

FEATURE 1 — FRAMING GAP
How much does the headline emotionally amplify or sensationalize the story relative to what the body actually reports?
- 0.0 = headline is a neutral, accurate summary of the body
- 0.3 = headline uses slightly stronger language than the body warrants
- 0.6 = headline foregrounds conflict/emotion that is minor or peripheral in the body
- 1.0 = headline is emotionally charged / alarming but the body is mundane or contradicts the tone

FEATURE 2 — CONFLICT VERB WITH ENTITY
Does a named entity (person, organization, or country) appear as the grammatical subject or object of a conflict verb?
Conflict verbs: attack, accuse, condemn, denounce, reject, clash, threaten, warn, defy, retaliate, escalate, sanction, expel, seize, invade, strike, bomb, protest, sue, arrest, charge, oust, ban.
Rules: direct usage only (not inside quotes), no metaphorical use, entity must be a named political/geopolitical actor.

FEATURE 3 — ACTOR IS SUBJECT
Identify the PRIMARY POLITICAL ACTOR (most prominent government, official, party, or geopolitical entity).
Determine whether that actor is framed predominantly as:
- agent: actor initiates or decides ("France announced", "the minister rejected")
- patient: actor receives action or is reacted upon ("France was criticized", "NATO was warned")
- mixed: roughly equal framing
Ignore quoted speech.

---

HEADLINE: "Le conducteur qui a écrasé le cycliste Paul Varry sera jugé pour meurtre"

BODY: "Au volant de son SUV, l’automobiliste avait percuté puis roulé sur le jeune homme après une altercation, le 15 octobre 2024, à Paris. Le parquet avait demandé qu’il soit renvoyé aux assises pour répondre d’homicide volontaire."


---

Respond ONLY with this exact JSON structure, no other text:
{
  "framing_gap": <float 0.0–1.0>,
  "conflict_verb_with_entity": <true|false>,
  "conflict_verb": "<verb or null>",
  "conflict_entity": "<entity or null>",
  "actor_is_subject": <true|false>,
  "primary_actor": "<name>",
  "actor_stance": "<agent|patient|mixed>",
  "actor_confidence": <float 0.0–1.0>
}
